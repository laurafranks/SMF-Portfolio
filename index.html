Profile

Stephen Franks is a 14 year veteran of the college system. During his time as a college professor Stephen has used his experience in the Information Technology industry to encourage and excite his students. He skilfully combines the Universal Design for Learning principles with authentic situations in his classes so his students graduate with the confidence to walk into a job and perform to the high expectations of today's employers.

As evident as Stephen's passion is for the industry he works in (he has had many careers, none of them lasted as long as his teaching career has) his passion for his students and their learning experience is even stronger. Stephen, coordinator of 3 programs in Durham College's School of Business, IT & Management, has worked closely with his team to bring fresh layout and equipment changes to the labs they live in for the benefit of their students. Labs have been refurbished with new desk layouts and equipment so students have a learning environment that resembles real world. In addition, relationships with industry partners like Cisco and local industries have introduced new and strong contacts and placement opportunities for students approaching their graduation.

Stephen's current focus is on working with his team to improve their use of Universal Design for Learning principles in their classes and developing a deeper and more sincere understanding of what authentic teaching and testing is and how to better and more consistently implement it in the classroom. Additionally, he is always exploring and excitedly introducing current technology to his students.

This sight is a testament to Stephen's passion for teaching, his experience, both in industry and in education, and the philosophy he brings to bare on his teaching and for his students. Please feel free to stay a while, explore his site, and feel free to contact him about his passions.
